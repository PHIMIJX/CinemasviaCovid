Impact of Streamimg,Lack of attendance and Outdated Movies

The follwing are the factors that have been observed to have had a huge impact on local cinemas world-wide.


Streamimg

Streaming is here and streaming is now, according to Deloitte streaming has taken over at least 47% of cinema revenue during the first lockdown alone.Although streaming is not exactly new, its influence and status truly evolved over the course of the pandemic as people could not “physically “go to the cinema.This has led to a decline in cinema attendance and an increase in "Home theater attendance" This has also resulted in people not being interested in the cinema even as lockdown eased.

Lack of attendance

With streaming as well as the pandemic keeping people at home, it can safely be said that cinemas have become a “ghost town”.Although cinemas have yet to be classified as a Super- spreader events,changesin prices have also affected attendance rate as well as people’s attitudetowards cinemas being the only providers of fresh new Block Busters.Even as Covid-19cases drop people have moved on to streaming and most have forgotten about cinemas

Outdated Movies

With cinemas being opened and closed on the regular, it has become difficult for them to acquire the latest movies.According to Deloitte,cinemas used to be at the top of the distribution chain but have now been completlydemoted as Box offices movies can now even be found on local Television (E. g DSTV)and most streaming platforms.

How Covid-19 has influenced Movie costs


How have movie costs changed?

According to my survey, the most common price for one movie ticket is now between the range of 50- 100.This is a slight increase from a movie ticket that was sold in 2019(Before COVID).


The increase was due to the amount of money lost during the course of the Lockdown period. When cinemas finally reopened, they like all businesses were forced to suffer the costs of new safety protocols.


A practical example of this is the new cleaning schedule implemented by Ster Kinekor. This involves cleaning before and after every movie screening.• The pandemic has also forced cinemas to adopt the use of ICT tools such as a QR scanner (Ster Kinekor uses QR scanners as way to scan movie tickets customers receive from the Ster Kinekor app) Costs have also prevented cinemas from acquiring popular safety protocols such as free masks.

Solution

The follwing are the solutions cinemas have developed in order to adopt to the pandemic


With this new age of pandemics and uncertainty cinemas have been forced to find new and safer ways to operate and generate revenue. Ster Kinekor for example has introduced the Ster Kinekor app. The app has been around for a while but has recently been optimized for use during the pandemic


When it comes to operations cinemas have yet to find alternative methods of delivering their service as people legitimately have to be at a cinema in order to view a movie. They have instead however decided to try to make a night out at the movies a much cleaner and Covid free experience.


Some cinemas such as Ster- Kinekor have introduced sanitation stations around the premises as well as sealed snacks and condiments in order to reduce the risk of food or area contamination. The have also increased cleaning cycles as well as introduce non-contact payments via the Ster Kinekor app. Lastly cinemas like most businesses have adapted to using social distancing indicators were applicable.
